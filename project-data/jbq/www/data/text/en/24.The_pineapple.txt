24.Because of her crown, she is the “Queen of the fruits”Originating from America and cultivated for thousands of year, the pineapple was naturally found in the day-to-day life in the pre-Columbian villages. They grew them in their gardens, they knew about their medicinal uses and they prepared beer and wine with her. Her original name was ananás, which means exquisite fruit in the Guarani language. The pineapple travels the world. When Columbus arrived in the New World the natives offered him the pineapple as a gesture of hospitality. Impressed by her flavour he brought her to back to Spain. She quickly found her way to Africa, Madagascar, China, India and the Philippines. Her arrival was so impactful in Europe that she instantly became a symbol of wealth.
